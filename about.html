9-20 Million Years Ago

The Common Ancestor

The last common ancestor of modern cats came from an ancient species known as Pseudaelurus that lived in Europe, Asia, and North America. Eventually modern cats evoloved from this species into eight groups.

10.8 Million Years Ago

The Pantheras

The oldest cat lineage is the Panthera lineage, also known as "the roaring cats" or the "big cats". It evolved into seven species who are found in multiple continents.

9.4 Million Years Ago

The Bay Cats

The Bay Cat lineage was next to evolve. It consists of three species that currently reside in southeast Asia.

8-10 Million Years Ago

Ice Age

An ice age occurs, creating many land bridges from frozen water, connecting many previously unreachable places.

8.5 million Years Ago

Caracals

Ancestors of the Caracals crossed the newly formed land bridges at the Red Sea from Asia and made their homes in Africa. They later evolved into three mid-sized cat species.

8 Million Years Ago

Leoparduses

The ancestors of the lineage at first evolved into two species in North America. Later during a second ice age, the Panama Isthmus land bridge between North and South America. The cats moved to South America and they became the seven species that we see today.

7.2 Million Years Ago

Lynxes

The lineage evolved into the lynx species, known for short tails and tufted ears. The first two species settled in North America. Two more popped up in Eurasia from ancestors that migrated back to Asia using the Bering Strait during the second ice age.

6.7 Million Years Ago

Pumas

Today the group has three species - the cheetah, puma (cougar) and jaguarundi. The puma and jaguarundi traveled to South America during the second ice age. The Cheetah migrated back to Eurasia and finally to Africa.

6.2 Million Years Ago

The Leopard Cats

The Leopard Cats branched off into five major species and currently occupy the regions Central and Southern Asia after they migrated from North America.

3.4 Million Years Ago

Felises

The Felis lineage mostly has smaller cats that weigh under 10 kgs. The domestic cat also comes from this lineage. These cats evolved in various continents, like North America, Asia, Africa, and Europe.
