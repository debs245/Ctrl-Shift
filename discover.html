Meet the Cats

Scroll down to explore the different species of wild cats sorted by the lineage they descend from. If a name catches your eye, click on it to be taken to a page where you can learn more!

Panthera

The Panthera Lineage includes some of the most popular and the largest cats in the cat family. Four of the seven cats (lion, tiger, jaguar, and leopard) are the only cats with the ability to roar. Unfortuantely, these cats also have the highest rate of endangerment for various reasons like hunters.

  1. Leopard

  2. Lion

  3. Jaguar

  4. Snow Leopard

  5. Tiger

  6. Clouded Leopard

  7. Sunda Clouded Leopard

Lynx

Lynxes are characterized by their very short tails and the black tufts of fur on the tip of their ears. They are most commonly found in the northern regions of the world, but can also be found in places like Mexico.

  1. Bobcat

  2. Canada Lynx

  3. Eurasian Lynx

  4. Iberian Lynx

Leopard Cat

Leopard cats (not to be confused with leopards) are found in Asia and live in wetland areas. They are also the second-youngest lineage out of the eight.

  1. Fishing Cat

  2. Leopard Cat

  3. Sunda Leopard Cat

  4. Flat-headed Cat

  5. Rusty-spotted Cat

  6. Pallas's Cat

Caracal

Cats from the caracal lineage are generally found in Africa, but each cat lives in a different type of habitat. The Caracals are also the 3rd oldest lineage, having diverged from the main line 8.5 million years ago.

  1. Serval

  2. African Golden Cat

  3. Caracal

Bay Cat

Bay cats live in the forests of southeast Asia. They're also the second oldest wild cat lineage, having diverged from the main line 9.4 million years ago.

  1. Asiatic Golden Cat

  2. Borneo Bay Cat

  3. Marbled Cat

Puma

The cats in the Puma Lineage are often called "big cats" due to their size, however, they do not have most of the qualities that big cats are known to have, like the ability to roar.

  1. Cougar

  2. Jaguarundi

  3. Cheetah

Felis

The Felis Lineage generally consists of the smallest cats in the cat family and is the youngest lineage ouyt of the eight. Domestic cats are also part of this lineage, though they're often not included on lists because they aren't "wild".

  1. Jungle cat

  2. Black-footed cat

  3. Sand cat

  4. Chinese mountain cat

  5. African wildcat

  6. European wildcat

  7. Domestic cat

Leopardus

Leopardus cats are found in Central and South America. They are particularly unique compared to their counterparts because they have 36 chromosomes while the other cats have 38.

  1. Geoffroy's cat

  2. Kodkod

  3. Oncilla

  4. Andean mountain cat

  5. Pampas cat

  6. Ocelot

  7. Margay
